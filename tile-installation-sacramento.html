Types of Tile Installation We Offer

Floor Tile Installation

Whether it's a kitchen, bathroom, entryway, or living room, we install tile floors that look flawless and last for decades. We work with all formats — from classic 12x12 ceramic to 48x96 large-format porcelain slabs — and every layout pattern including straight set, offset/brick, diagonal, herringbone, and custom designs.

Proper floor prep is non-negotiable for us. We check for deflection, install cement backer board or Schluter Ditra where needed, and use premium thin-set mortars appropriate for the tile type. This is why our floors don't crack, pop, or show lippage after a year.

Wall Tile Installation

Wall tile in Sacramento homes runs the gamut from classic subway tile in bathrooms to full-height natural stone in open-concept kitchens. We handle all of it — ensuring level, plumb surfaces, consistent grout joints, and proper waterproofing behind wet areas.

Large-Format Tile

Large-format tile (anything over 15" on the short side) requires specialized skills, equipment, and mortars. We're experienced with slabs up to 60x120, including proper back-buttering, lippage management, and the additional surface prep these tiles demand.

Custom Pattern Tile Work

Herringbone, Versailles, windmill, pinwheel, basketweave — custom layouts take precision layout planning and patience. We start every custom pattern with a dry layout to dial in the look before any mortar hits the floor. Sacramento homeowners hire us specifically for this kind of work when other contractors say it's too complicated.

Our Installation Process

Every tile installation with Pro Tiling follows the same disciplined process:

  • Free on-site estimate and project assessment
  • Surface inspection — checking for level, structural integrity, and moisture
  • Demo and debris removal (if applicable)
  • Surface prep: backer board, waterproofing membranes, leveling compounds
  • Layout planning — always a dry run before mortar
  • Tile setting with appropriate mortar and trowel technique
  • Curing period before grouting
  • Grout installation and sealing
  • Final cleanup and walkthrough
