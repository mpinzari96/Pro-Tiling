Kitchen Backsplash Experts

A Backsplash That Actually Makes the Kitchen

The backsplash is one of the most visible surfaces in any kitchen — and one of the most underinvested. A well-chosen and expertly installed backsplash can tie together countertops, cabinets, and appliances in a way that makes the whole room feel intentional and complete.

Pro Tiling has installed kitchen backsplashes throughout Sacramento, Roseville, Elk Grove, and Folsom — from simple 3-inch subway tile for classic kitchens to full-slab porcelain behind a range for a high-end contemporary look. We bring the same attention to detail to a backsplash job that we do to a full bathroom remodel.

Backsplash Styles We Install

Classic Subway Tile Backsplash

Never goes out of style. We install subway tile in all sizes — 3x6, 4x8, 4x12 — in traditional brick offset, stacked vertical, herringbone, and horizontal stack patterns. The right grout color and joint size changes the whole character of the installation, and we help you dial those choices in during planning.

Full-Height Backsplash

Running tile all the way from countertop to upper cabinet — or all the way to the ceiling — is a popular upgrade in Sacramento kitchen remodels. It elongates the space, eliminates the painted drywall band, and gives the kitchen a more custom, built-in feel.

Large-Format Slab Backsplash

A single-slab or large-tile backsplash with minimal grout lines creates a sleek, modern look that's also easier to keep clean. We install porcelain and natural stone slabs behind ranges and along full runs with precision-cut openings for outlets and switches.

Mosaic & Patterned Tile Backsplash

Zellige, encaustic cement tile, glass mosaic, and hand-painted patterned tile all require extra care to install correctly — especially around the edges and where patterns need to be centered and balanced. This is exactly the type of custom tile work we specialize in.

Range Hood Focal Tile

The area directly behind and above a range is the focal point of the kitchen. We design and install feature tile in this zone — whether that's a dramatic natural stone, a patterned cement tile, or a contrasting material that makes the range hood stand out.

What Makes Our Backsplash Installs Different

On a backsplash, details matter more than almost anywhere else — because you're looking directly at it every day. We take time to:

  • Center the layout visually, not just mathematically
  • Cut around outlets, switches, and range hoods cleanly
  • Use the correct adhesive for the tile type and substrate
  • Keep grout joints consistent throughout
  • Caulk all inside corners (not grout them) for long-term durability
  • Leave zero adhesive residue on tile faces

These are the small things that separate a pro install from an amateur one — and they're the things Sacramento homeowners notice every single day.
